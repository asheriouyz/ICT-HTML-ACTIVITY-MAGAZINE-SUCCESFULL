Editor's Note

In today's rapidly evolving digital world, teenagers are becoming increasingly exposed to social media, which plays a significant role in shaping their daily lives. While platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and Snapchat can provide opportunities for connection and self-expression, excessive use can also have negative effects on mental and emotional well-being. Studies show that too much social media exposure is linked to higher levels of anxiety, depression, and lower self-esteem among teens (Pew Research Center, 2022). It can also disrupt sleep patterns, reduce attention spans, and encourage unhealthy comparisons with peers (American Psychological Association, 2021). Moreover, constant engagement online may limit real-life social interactions, affecting communication skills and emotional resilience. Understanding these effects is crucial for helping teens navigate the digital landscape safely and responsibly.

By understanding these effects, teens can make more informed choices about how they use social media, setting boundaries that protect their mental and emotional well-being. Mindful engagement allows them to enjoy the benefits of digital platforms—such as learning, creativity, and self-expression—without falling into unhealthy patterns or comparisons. Developing healthy online habits can also encourage positive real-world interactions, stronger focus, and a sense of balance in daily life. I hope this issue inspires readers to continue exploring, creating, and sharing their ideas, turning social media into a tool for growth rather than a source of stress.
